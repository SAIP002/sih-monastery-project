Events & Festivals

Cultural calendar and festival highlights

Important Events

Saga Dawa

Saga Dawa is one of the holiest Buddhist festivals in Sikkim, observed on the full moon of the fourth Tibetan lunar month, usually in May or June. It commemorates the three most significant events in Lord Buddha’s life – his birth, enlightenment, and nirvana. On this day, monks in monasteries chant sacred prayers while devotees visit stupas and monasteries, circumambulating them with prayer wheels and flags. People light butter lamps, burn incense, and give offerings, while many practice vegetarianism and perform good deeds to earn merit. The festival creates a deeply spiritual atmosphere across Sikkim, reminding people of compassion and kindness.

Pang Lhabsol

Pang Lhabsol is a unique festival of Sikkim, celebrated in August or September on the fifteenth day of the seventh Tibetan lunar month. It is dedicated to Mount Kanchenjunga, revered as the guardian deity of Sikkim, and symbolizes the unity and protection of the land. The highlight of the festival is the vibrant masked Cham dances performed by monks in monasteries, including the famous warrior dance called Pang-Toed Chaam, which represents bravery and the protection of the people. Devotees offer prayers to Mount Kanchenjunga for good harvests, safety, and peace. Beyond its religious importance, Pang Lhabsol also serves as a reminder of harmony among the Lepcha, Bhutia, and Nepali communities of Sikkim..

Losar

Losar, the Tibetan New Year, is one of the most colorful and lively festivals celebrated in Sikkim. Falling in February or March, it marks the beginning of the Tibetan lunar calendar. In preparation, homes and monasteries are cleaned and decorated to welcome positive energies for the new year. Families cook traditional dishes such as khapse (fried pastries) and chang (barley beer), while people wear new clothes and exchange greetings with loved ones. Monasteries hold special prayer ceremonies to invoke blessings for peace and prosperity. Cultural programs, folk dances, and social gatherings make this festival a time of joy, unity, and new beginnings for the Sikkimese people.
